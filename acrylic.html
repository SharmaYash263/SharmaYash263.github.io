Acrylic Art Pieces

This page presents a timeline of my journey with acrylic painting, highlighting the evolution of my skills and techniques. Each painting marks a significant step in my artistic development, showcasing how my style and approach to using acrylics have matured over time.

2020 | 2019 | 2018 | Pre-2016

2020

David in Pop

"David in Pop" created on August 7, 2020, employs a mix of acrylics, pencil, and glaze. This piece captures the iconic image of David, repeated four times in vibrant pop art style, evoking a sense of modernity and boldness. The blending of bright, contrasting colors creates a dynamic visual effect, highlighting the intricate details and unique expressions in each rendition of David. The repetition and variation in color and style draw the viewer into the scene, making each version of David stand out in its own right. The vibrant palette not only brings a modern twist to the classical subject but also emphasizes the bold and playful nature of pop art.

2019

The Great Wave off Kanagawa

"The Great Wave off Kanagawa" created on September 2, 2019, employs a mix of acrylics, pencil, and glaze. This piece captures the dramatic power and beauty of the iconic wave, evoking a sense of awe and respect for nature's force. The blending of blues and whites creates a dynamic visual effect, highlighting the intricate details and movement of the water. This painting not only pays homage to the original, but is a testament to my ability to recreate other paintings and media with precision and detail.

Tribal Lion

"Tribal Lion" created in 2019, employs a mix of acrylics, pencil, and glaze. It captures the powerful essence of a tribal lion against the backdrop of a stunning sunset gradient, evoking a sense of strength and mystique. The blending of warm colors creates a dynamic visual effect, highlighting the tribal patterns and the lion's presence.

2018

Shooting Star

"Shooting Star" created in 2018, employs a mix of acrylics, pencil, and glaze. It captures the brilliance and fleeting beauty of a shooting star streaking across the night sky, evoking a sense of wonder and awe. The blending of vibrant colors creates a dynamic visual effect, reflecting the mystery of this celestial phenomenon.

"DKNY" created in 2018, uses acrylics, pencil, and glaze. My family often signs off messages and cards as DKNY, making this piece especially meaningful. The blending of colors creates a striking visual effect, with each design customized for each family member. Each section of the painting reflects the unique personality and preferences of my family members, making it a deeply personal and cherished decoration within our home, highlighting the individuality of each family member in the piece.

Pre-2016

Ganesha

"Ganesha" created in 2014, uses acrylics, pencil, and glaze. In this piece, I focused on dynamic brush strokes and clear visualization. The blending of browns, golds, and copper, contrasted with bright red, creates a warm color palette that complements the Hindu god Ganesha.

Swirling Skies

"Swirling Skies" created in 2013, uses acrylics, pencil, and glaze. In this piece, I aimed to explore creative liberties and improvisation within the sky's scenes. The swirling clouds required careful color choices, adding depth and dynamism to the intense sunset over the savannah.

Safari Sunset

"Safari Sunset" created in 2011, uses acrylics, pencil, and glaze. It captures the intense heat of an overbearing sun setting over the savannah, evoking the warmth and beauty of an African evening.

Beachside

"Beachside" created in 2010, uses acrylic paints, pencil, and acrylic glaze. This painting was my first attempt with acrylic paint and my first time using a canvas. It features precise lines and a luminous finish, capturing a serene beach scene.
